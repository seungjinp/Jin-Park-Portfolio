Exploring Self Representation on Dating Apps

Case study examining how users represent themselves on mobile dating apps in relation to their purpose on the app.

→ view final paper

Project brief & my role

This case study was the final deliverable for INFO 4450: Computer-Mediated Communication class at Cornell University. The project explores how users represent themselves on mobile dating apps in relation to their purpose on the app.

‍

team & timeline

From March 2021 to May 2021, I worked with 6 other teammates to finish the project.

reflection

In our results, we present information about participants’ self-representation behaviors with respect to their relationship preferences and how these behaviors may or may not vary across different dating apps. We assert that the relationships between the variables (eg. male online daters and physical attractiveness, etc.) of our hypotheses are to be considered associative and not causal. This was a fun project for all of us, as we all have used an online dating app at least once during our times at Cornell! 😆
