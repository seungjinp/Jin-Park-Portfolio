Project brief & my role

This case study was the final deliverable for INFO 4940: Technology for Underserved Communities class at Cornell University. The project examines how censored communities can mobilize via technology and share in- formation safely during political protests without government intervention or attacks from bad actors. We propose four recommendations for future researchers and designers to consider when developing technology to better support underserved communities for mobilization.

team & timeline

From March 2021 to May 2021, I worked with 3 teammates to finish the project. In this paper, we analyze the use of technology during political movements through four case studies: the Iranian Green Movement (2009), Arab Spring (2010-2012), Black Lives Matter (2013-), and Umbrella Movement (2014).
