Project brief & my role

Whereabouts was the final project for INFO 4120: Ubiquitous Computing class at Cornell University. The project discusses how movement data can be collected and framed on a mobile phone platform, so it can help students understand and quantify symptoms of stress. I was responsible for developing an algorithm that clusters location data used to support an application that visualizes data and creates custom feedback for the user. I also contributed to the UX¬†design of the prototype of this mobile intervention, which was made as a proof of concept of how movement data can indicate stress, and how proper data framing can bring mental health awareness.

Our research delves into the product design and implementation of a platform named ‚ÄúWhereabouts‚Äù. This platform leverages mobile device sensors to collect location data of a user and to build a stronger image of their mental health wellbeing. We aim to find a relationship between location movement and stress and to help Cornell students bring awareness to their own stress and mood and what healthy activities help them cope.

Research process & design goals

We hypothesize that mental health wellbeing is significantly related to routine physical movement from different locations. The locational movement can help indicate the health of a student, such as, changes in routine, increased visits to a favorite place, and extreme amounts of decreased movement. Additionally, we predict that a platform that can provide an indication of users‚Äô mental well-being through analysis of locational data, will allow users to self-reflect more effectively about their own wellbeing in real time.

‚Äç

Our study is broken into three separate investigations.

1. What relationship exists between location movement and student stress? Does there exist a significant relationship between physical activity and stress? If so, what is the correlation?

2. How can location data be collected from a mobile device and processed to predict a general state of mental health? If stress is correlated to movement, how can we design an algorithm that will help collect such data? We will investigate how sensor data may be collected and processed to produce predictions of mental wellbeing.

3. How can movement information be framed so that our app is an effective intervention? Lastly, what type of information are students looking for in a mental health awareness application? How can an application be best designed to develop a consistent and reliable repertoire with its users?
