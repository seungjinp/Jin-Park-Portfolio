The student dashboard project was the final deliverable for INFO 4100: Learning Analytics class at Cornell University. The project required us to develope a learning analytics dashboard based on clicker data using R¬†Shiny package.

team & timeline

From October 2020 to December 2020, I worked with 6 other teammates to finish the project. Our responsibilities included understanding the structure of clicker data, creating multiple different visualizations, and designing and implementing an instructor and student dashboard.

Scenario

You are approached by a college instructor who uses iClickers in her CS class on Business Intelligence. She would like to gain insights about her students and how they are engaging/performing in order to better help them in class. She would also like to better support students by giving them feedback at scale about where they stand and perhaps how they compare to others in the class. You offer to build a prototype of a dashboard using her clicker data: this is a dashboard for the instructor which offers an overview of the class characteristics, engagement, and performance; and it is a dashboard for students which offers a specific student an overview of their engagement and performance (and how it compares to others).

outcome

We really liked how our dashboard provided students with a succinct overview of their course performance and engagement at the top and how important individual-level data was apparent. Students can clearly recognize information in regards to (1) their individual performance (2) course engagement and participation, and (3) course experience and attitude. This snapshot of average participation, quiz, and temperature scores gives the student a clear understanding of their standing in the class. We also liked that our dashboard was interactive in that it provided personalized information depending on the Student ID selected on the sidebar. The plots also give students a clear understanding of how their performance, engagement, and experience in the class compare to other students. Students can also keep track of their performance over time through a clear line graph that shows their quiz scores throughout the sessions.
